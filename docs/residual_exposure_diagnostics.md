# Residual And Exposure Diagnostics

Run `python scripts/analyze_residual_exposure_diagnostics.py` after Provo strict-line conversion. The generated JSON is the authoritative complete result and the CSV retains partition-level reliability rows.

The audit covers syntax and flexible nuisance specifications. Full-sample five-fold text-crossfit predictions produce Pearson residuals `(y-E)/sqrt(E(1-p))`, raw deviations `y-E`, and signed cell-deviance residuals `sign(y-E) sqrt(2[y log(y/E)-(y-E)])`. The implementation defines `0 log(0/E)=0` and marks positive observations with zero expectation, and Pearson cells with zero conditional variance, undefined. Cellwise deviance contributions sum in square to group multinomial deviance, but signs and individual cells are descriptive: multinomial cells are dependent, and this is neither an independent-Poisson residual nor a unique scalar multinomial decomposition.

Reliability uses the original 100 fixed 42/42 reader partitions and reports text-equal medians plus descriptive edge-weighted values, defined texts, and eligible edges. Expected-count strata are `<0.5`, `0.5-1`, `1-5`, and `>=5`; source-exposure strata are also reported. There are no edge-level significance tests.

Exposure matching preserves the full candidate universe. For each subject separately, adjacent and near-nonadjacent integer counts are independently binomial-thinned toward that subject's far-category total mass; far counts are unchanged. Thinned subject arrays are then aggregated using the original halves. The original half-fitted nuisance probabilities are reused, avoiding a second fit and isolating a measurement-mass perturbation. One deterministic draw is made for each of at least 100 partitions. This is not exact exposure conditioning: it adds binomial noise, cannot increase far information, and subjects with zero far mass yield zero retained short-distance mass. Defined texts and edges quantify resulting failures.

Interpretation is deliberately limited. A short-distance advantage that survives thinning is evidence that observed reproducibility is not explained solely by aggregate category mass under this perturbation. Its disappearance would show mass/sparsity suffices to explain the observed contrast under thinning. Neither outcome identifies latent category structure, and low far reliability means only low observed reproducibility under sparse measurement.

The `<5` stratum is intentionally undefined because reliability requires exposure `>=5`; this is explicitly represented by zero defined texts and edges rather than silently omitted.

The category audit makes the sparsity contrast explicit. Syntax mean/median expected counts are `26.655/23.095` adjacent, `13.444/8.711` near, and `.442/.094` far; flexible values are `26.653/23.174`, `13.512/9.002`, and `.414/.069`. After subject-category mass thinning, syntax Pearson medians are `.9087` adjacent, `.5691` near, and `.4593` far; flexible values are `.9093`, `.5586`, and `.4087`. Syntax deviance values are `.8189/.4458/.6185`, while flexible values are `.8061/.4240/.6067`; thus the ordering is residual-dependent. Raw values are `.8820/.7251/.8005` and `.8765/.7071/.8005`. Median defined texts/edges are `33/117` adjacent, `47/229.5` near, and `46/729` far. Adjacent therefore loses 22 of 55 text correlations and the matched results have materially different defined-cell support. Aggregate category mass matching does not match per-edge expected count because far has 10,119 candidates versus 2,468 adjacent and 4,182 near. The Pearson short-distance ordering remains among defined texts, but deviance and raw results do not support a monotonic adjacent-near-far ordering. The defensible conclusion is residual-scale sensitivity plus higher observed short-distance Pearson reproducibility under this thinning perturbation, not weaker far latent structure.

## Source Eligibility Sensitivity

Source inclusion is outcome-conditioned because the primary pattern-correlation rule requires at least five observed source events in each half. The audit reused half-specific nuisance probabilities for all 100 fixed seeded reader partitions and changed only the eligibility rule. Syntax Pearson edge-pattern medians were `.4648`, `.4650`, `.4653`, and `.4764` at minimum half exposures `1`, `3`, `5`, and `10`; a mask defined only by full-84 exposure at least `10` yielded `.4648`. Flexible values were `.4234`, `.4234`, `.4231`, and `.4344`, with `.4235` under the full-84-only mask. Full edge-count distributions are retained in the canonical JSON/CSV.

The primary threshold of five was close to less selective and full-aggregate-mask estimates in this 100-partition audit. The modest increase at threshold ten shows descriptively that stricter outcome-conditioned selection can raise observed correlation. This sensitivity does not eliminate selection, provide population inference, or replace a hierarchical model.
